STEM Architect Master Guide

Future Focus: The Complete 50-Module STEM Career Exploration Series (Enhanced Field Edition)

This comprehensive curriculum provides a high-velocity, modular learning series consisting of 50 micro-learning units (15-20 minutes each). Designed for Texas TRIO programs (Upward Bound/UBMS), it covers a vast spectrum of STEM professions from Genetics to Petroleum Engineering. The series utilizes low-cost, hands-on simulations to expose high school students to diverse career pathways and collegiate majors available in the Texas university system. This edition is enhanced with a robust field expedition strategy focused on the DFW Metroplex to bridge classroom theory with real-world application.

Target: High School (Ages 14-18) • Total Time: 25-30 Hours (Total for 50 Modules + Field Expeditions) • Est. Cost: $25.54

Curriculum Narrative: Future Focus: The Complete 50-Module STEM Career Exploration Series (Enhanced Field Edition)

Executive Summary

Future Focus: The Complete 50-Module STEM Career Exploration Series is a high-velocity, modular curriculum designed specifically for Texas TRIO Upward Bound and Upward Bound Math-Science programs. The series consists of 50 micro-learning units that bridge the gap between secondary education and high-demand STEM careers. By utilizing low-cost, high-impact simulations, the program exposes students to diverse professional pathways ranging from Genetic Counseling to Petroleum Engineering. The curriculum is further strengthened by a strategic field expedition component centered in the Dallas-Fort Worth Metroplex, allowing students to witness the practical application of classroom theories in world-class facilities and collegiate environments.

Compliance & Alignment

DOE TRIO Compliance Strategy

This curriculum is meticulously aligned with the allowable costs and services outlined in the Higher Education Act (HEA) of 1965, specifically Section 402C (Upward Bound) and Section 402E (UBMS). Activities fulfill the federal mandate for providing 'instruction in core academic subjects' and 'exposure to academic programs and cultural events' (34 CFR 645.11). Furthermore, all associated instructional supplies and equipment are classified as allowable costs under the Uniform Administrative Requirements, Cost Principles, and Audit Requirements for Federal Awards (2 CFR 200.453 and 200.439), as they are necessary and reasonable for the successful delivery of STEM-focused college preparatory services. The project-based nature of the modules supports the TRIO mission of increasing post-secondary enrollment and completion rates among first-generation and low-income students by providing rigorous academic enrichment.

Standards Alignment

The Future Focus series is mapped directly to the Texas Essential Knowledge and Skills (TEKS) across multiple disciplines, including Biology (¬ß112.34), Chemistry (¬ß112.35), Physics (¬ß112.39), and Technology Applications (¬ß126.32). Each module is designed to support the Texas STEM Framework by fostering 'STEM Fluency Skills' such as critical thinking, communication, and solution-finding. By integrating standards-based science and math applications with career-specific competencies, the curriculum ensures that students remain competitive in the Texas university system and the broader workforce.

Field Expeditions

The 'Enhanced Field Edition' includes a curated itinerary of 10 site visits located within 60 miles of Dallas, Tarrant, and Johnson counties. These expeditions provide students with direct access to STEM leaders, including Mouser Electronics for logistics, the University of Texas at Arlington for research-tier laboratory exposure, and Lockheed Martin for aerospace manufacturing. Each site visit is selected for its ability to reinforce specific curriculum modules‚Äîsuch as the American Airlines CR Smith Museum for aerospace mechanics‚Äîwhile fulfilling the TRIO mandate for exposing students to both professional work environments and four-year university campuses in the North Texas region.

Module Narratives

Module 01: The Genetic Code Breaker

Students step into the role of Genetic Counselors, engaging in a rigorous simulation of the Central Dogma of biology. By transcribing DNA sequences into mRNA and translating them into amino acids using codon charts, students identify specific genetic mutations. This activity emphasizes the precision required in health science and the clinical relevance of molecular biology.

Module 02: Cybersecurity Sentinel

This module introduces students to the high-stakes field of information security through the application of cryptography. Using Caesar Cipher wheels, students learn the logic of encryption and decryption while performing a security audit on mock digital profiles to identify vulnerabilities, mirroring the day-to-day operations of a SOC Analyst.

Module 03: The 10-Minute Bridge

Focusing on Civil Engineering, students face a structural challenge that requires the application of geometric principles and physics. By building truss systems with limited materials, students observe the distribution of tension and compression, learning how triangulation provides structural integrity in modern infrastructure.

Module 04: The Solar Sprint

Students explore Electrical Engineering and renewable energy by measuring the voltage output of solar cells. By manipulating the angle of incidence and measuring results with multimeters, they analyze the efficiency of the Texas energy grid, connecting physical science standards to the growing green-energy workforce.

Module 05: The Hydraulic Hand

Applying Pascal‚Äôs Law, students construct a mechanical joint powered by fluid dynamics. This hands-on robotics module demonstrates the difference between hydraulic and pneumatic systems, providing a foundational understanding of the industrial automation used in North Texas manufacturing hubs.

Module 06: Patient Zero

Acting as Epidemiologists, students use pH indicators to model the transmission of a pathogen through a population. The activity requires rigorous logic and contact tracing to identify 'Patient Zero,' teaching students the mathematical and biological foundations of public health management.

Module 07: The Ink Detective

This Forensic Science module utilizes paper chromatography to separate chemical pigments in evidence samples. Students calculate retention factors and analyze solubility, mimicking the investigative techniques used in criminal justice laboratories to match physical evidence to suspects.

Module 08: The Perfect Fin

Students delve into Aerospace Engineering by designing and testing aerodynamic stabilizers for rockets. By manipulating the center of pressure and observing flight stability, students apply Newton‚Äôs Laws of Motion to real-world flight mechanics, preparing them for the local aerospace job market.

Module 09: The Phlebotomy Simulation

This health-focused module introduces students to immunology and clinical procedures. Through a simulated blood typing exercise, students learn about antigen-antibody reactions and the critical importance of donor compatibility, providing a gateway into nursing and laboratory science careers.

Module 10: Graphite Circuits

Using the conductive properties of graphite, students draw functional electrical circuits on paper. This activity provides an intuitive introduction to circuit design and resistance, allowing students to visualize the flow of current before advancing to more complex electrical engineering concepts.

Module 11: The Water Filter Challenge

Students act as Environmental Engineers to design a multi-stage mechanical filtration system. By analyzing the permeability of different materials like sand and charcoal, they learn the science of water treatment and the importance of ecological conservation in urban planning.

Module 12: Binary Bracelets

This Computer Science module translates abstract machine code into a tangible form. By encoding their initials into 8-bit binary strings using colored beads, students master the base-2 number system that forms the foundation of all modern computing and data storage.

Module 13: Algorithm Maze

Students explore the fundamentals of software engineering by creating sequential logic commands for a 'human robot.' This screen-free activity emphasizes the importance of syntax, debugging, and computational thinking in the development of robust software algorithms.

Module 14: The Origami Telescope

Merging geometry with mechanical engineering, students replicate the complex folding mechanisms used in satellite deployment. By mastering the Miura fold, they learn how aerospace engineers maximize payload space, a direct link to the advanced manufacturing sectors in the DFW area.

Module 15: Acid Rain Detective

In this geochemistry lab, students observe the chemical weathering of calcium carbonate when exposed to acidic solutions. The experiment simulates the environmental impact of industrial pollution on geological structures, teaching students to analyze chemical reactions in the context of environmental science.

Module 16: The Catapult Siege

Students explore the conversion of elastic potential energy into kinetic energy by constructing a popsicle stick catapult. This module requires students to optimize their design for trajectory and accuracy, reinforcing physics concepts related to projectile motion and mechanical advantage.

Module 17: Cloud in a Bottle

This meteorology simulation demonstrates the relationship between pressure and temperature. By rapidly depressurizing a vessel, students create a visible condensation cloud, allowing them to visualize the thermodynamic principles that drive weather patterns and atmospheric changes.

Module 18: Heart Rate Logic

Students engage in a cardiovascular health assessment by measuring and analyzing pulse rates during rest and activity. This module combines biology with statistics, as students calculate cardiac output and recovery times, exploring careers in cardiology and sports medicine.

Module 19: Polymer Slime

This chemical engineering module focuses on the synthesis of non-Newtonian fluids. By cross-linking polymers through a reaction between PVA glue and borax, students witness the transformation of matter at the molecular level, gaining insight into the plastics and materials science industries.

Module 20: Soil pH Analysis

Students apply agricultural science by testing soil samples for acidity and alkalinity. Using chemical indicators to determine soil viability for specific crops, students learn how chemistry and geology intersect to support the massive Texas agricultural sector.

Module 21: Sound Wave Straws

Focusing on acoustical engineering, students construct pan flutes to explore the relationship between pipe length and sound frequency. This activity demonstrates the physics of waves and resonance, showing how engineers manipulate physical properties to produce specific auditory results.

Module 22: The Lens Lab

Students explore the physics of optics by creating liquid lenses. By observing how the curvature of a water drop refracts light to magnify text, they learn the foundational principles used by optometrists and optical engineers to design corrective eyewear and imaging technology.

Module 23: The Network Packet Game

This physical simulation models how information travels across the internet via TCP/IP protocols. Students act as routers, navigating 'packets' through potential bottlenecks and link failures, gaining a systems-level understanding of IT infrastructure and network administration.

Module 24: Gear Ratios

Students investigate the mechanical advantage of gear trains, focusing on the trade-off between torque and speed. By calculating gear ratios and observing the rotations of connected components, they gain a practical understanding of the transmission systems used in automotive engineering.

Module 25: Paper Strength

In this materials science module, students perform stress tests on paper strips to determine their failure points. By comparing the tensile strength of dry versus saturated fibers, students learn how industrial engineers select and test materials for specific environmental conditions.

Module 26: Probability Dice

Students delve into data science by generating a bell curve through repeated trials of dice rolls. This activity teaches the basics of statistical distribution and probability, which are essential for careers in actuarial science, risk management, and big-data analytics.

Module 27: Density Tower

Applying the formula for density (Mass/Volume), students create a multi-layered tower of immiscible liquids. This module connects chemistry to oceanography, as students learn how stratification in the ocean creates distinct environments based on salinity and temperature.

Module 28: City Zoning Map

Students act as Urban Planners to design a town grid that balances residential needs with industrial requirements. This activity emphasizes logic and environmental systems, as students must mitigate the impact of traffic and pollution through strategic land-use planning.

Module 29: Serial Dilution

This module introduces students to the precision of pharmacy compounding. By performing a step-wise dilution of colored solutions, students learn to calculate parts-per-million and percentages, reinforcing the mathematical rigor required in medical and pharmaceutical careers.

Module 30: Oil & Water Emulsion

Students explore the chemistry of food science by creating a stable emulsion using surfactants. By understanding the interaction between hydrophilic and hydrophobic molecules, students learn how chemical engineering is applied to the production of consumer goods and shelf-stable foods.

Module 31: Jello Shake Table

Module 32: Parallax Stars

Students use triangulation and the parallax effect to measure the distance of remote objects. This astronomy-focused module applies high-level geometry to help students understand how scientists calculate the vast distances between stars and galaxies.

Module 33: Data Viz Challenge

This module teaches students to transform raw data into compelling visual narratives. By choosing the correct chart types for various data sets, students learn the art and science of business intelligence, becoming proficient in communicating complex information to a non-technical audience.

Module 35: Leaf Transpiration

This botany lab allows students to observe the water cycle in action by capturing vapor released from a live leaf. This activity demonstrates the role of plants in regulating atmospheric moisture and highlights the importance of environmental scientists in studying climate change.

Module 36: Rock Sponges

Students model the geological properties of oil reservoirs using sponges to understand porosity and permeability. This petroleum engineering module demonstrates how fluid is extracted from solid rock, providing context for the energy sector that is vital to the Texas economy.

Module 39: Triangulation

Using protractors and map baselines, students practice the surveying techniques used by civil engineers and cartographers. This geometry-intensive module shows how angles and distance are used to pinpoint locations on Earth, mirroring the logic used by GPS technology.
