STEM Architect Master Guide

Essential Chemistry: A 40-Module High School Lab Series (Texas TRIO Edition)

A comprehensive, hands-on chemistry curriculum designed for Texas High School students (grades 9-12). This guide structures 40 short-format modules (20-40 minutes each) focusing on foundational concepts ranging from properties of matter to chemical kinetics, stoichiometry, and organic chemistry. Explicitly designed for compliance with Texas TEKS (Chapter 112.35) and Federal TRIO (Upward Bound/UBMS) grant regulations (34 CFR Part 645), emphasizing low-cost consumables (EDGAR 2 CFR 200.453) and high-engagement inquiry.

Target: High School (Ages 14-18) • Total Time: 20-25 Hours (40 Modules x 30-40 mins) • Est. Cost: $470.00

Duration: 30 minutes TRIO: Both

Students investigate the physical property of density by creating a multi-layered density column using household liquids with varying sugar concentrations.

Learning Objectives

Essential Chemistry: A 40-Module High School Lab Series (Texas TRIO Edition) - Curriculum Narrative

Executive Summary

Essential Chemistry: A 40-Module High School Lab Series (Texas TRIO Edition) is an inquiry-based laboratory curriculum designed to enhance the scientific literacy and college readiness of high school students. Spanning 40 modules, the curriculum bridges fundamental chemical concepts with real-world applications, emphasizing hands-on experimentation and critical thinking. Designed specifically for the educational landscape of North Texas, this series integrates rigorous academic standards with career-focused STEM pathways, ensuring that students from underrepresented backgrounds are equipped with the technical skills and theoretical knowledge required for success in post-secondary laboratory sciences and engineering disciplines.

Compliance & Alignment

DOE TRIO Compliance Strategy

This curriculum is meticulously structured to align with the Higher Education Act (HEA) Sections 402C and 402E, which mandate academic instruction in laboratory science for Upward Bound (UB) and Upward Bound Math-Science (UBMS) projects. Specifically, the modules satisfy 34 CFR Part 645.11(a)(5) and 645.11(b)(1) by providing rigorous instructional services that improve student competency in core science subjects. All materials required for these activities are compliant with EDGAR and 2 CFR 200.453 cost principles, focusing on allowable instructional supplies that are necessary and reasonable for the delivery of the program‚Äôs academic goals. By prioritizing low-cost consumables and scalable experiments, the program maintains fiscal efficiency while meeting the federal requirement to expose students to a college-preparatory science curriculum.

Standards Alignment

The Essential Chemistry series is fully aligned with the Texas Essential Knowledge and Skills (TEKS) for Chemistry as outlined in 19 TAC Chapter 112.35. Each module is cross-referenced with specific student expectations, including the investigation of physical and chemical properties (c)(4), atomic theory (c)(6), chemical reactions (c)(8), and gas laws (c)(9). Furthermore, the curriculum supports the Texas STEM Framework by promoting employability skills such as collaborative problem-solving, data analysis, and scientific communication, ensuring students meet the high bar set for secondary science education in the state of Texas.

Field Expeditions

Strategic experiential learning is integrated through field trips within a 60-mile radius of Dallas, Tarrant, and Johnson counties, providing students with exposure to high-level STEM environments. Highlights include the Perot Museum‚Äôs Gems and Minerals Hall in Dallas for crystallography study, the Bureau of Engraving and Printing in Fort Worth for industrial ink chemistry, the Trinity River Authority for environmental filtration processes, and the UTA Shimadzu Center for Advanced Analytical Chemistry for exposure to college-level spectroscopy. these trips are selected to reinforce classroom modules while introducing students to professional chemical and environmental engineering career paths.

Module Narratives

Module 1: Liquid Layers - Mastering Density

Students investigate the physical property of density by creating a multi-layered density column. This activity emphasizes precision in volumetric measurement and pipetting, requiring students to calculate density based on mass/volume ratios. It introduces the rigor of quantitative observation essential for careers in oceanography and chemical engineering.

Module 2: Foaming Fury - Catalysts in Action

This module explores kinetics through the rapid decomposition of hydrogen peroxide. Students observe the role of catalysts and identify exothermic reactions by monitoring temperature changes. The activity bridges the gap between theoretical reaction rates and industrial chemical processes.

Module 3: The Color of Chemistry - Acids and Bases

Using natural pH indicators, students classify household substances on the pH scale. This lab reinforces the concepts of ionization and neutralization, grounding logarithmic chemistry in familiar substances while introducing environmental testing protocols.

Module 4: Gas Laws - The Crushing Can

Students experience the power of atmospheric pressure and Charles's Law through a dramatic phase-change demonstration. This activity connects the kinetic molecular theory to mechanical engineering and HVAC career pathways.

Module 5: Slime Time - Introduction to Polymers

This module focuses on materials science by synthesizing cross-linked polymers. Students examine how molecular structures dictate physical properties, providing a foundation for understanding synthetic materials and modern product development.

Module 6: Chromatographic Colors - Separation Science

Students apply paper chromatography to separate pigments based on molecular polarity. This lab introduces the fundamental concepts of mobile and stationary phases, used extensively in forensic science and pharmaceutical purification.

Module 7: Flame Tests - Atomic Fingerprints

Through the observation of atomic emission spectra, students identify metallic ions by their characteristic flame colors. This module connects quantum mechanics to qualitative analysis, simulating techniques used by astronomers and analytical chemists.

Module 8: Bag of Change - Conservation of Mass

Students conduct a closed-system reaction to verify the Law of Conservation of Mass. This experiment requires meticulous weighing and data recording, reinforcing the foundational accounting principles of chemistry.

Module 9: Hot and Cold - Energy in Reactions

By comparing the dissolution of ammonium nitrate and calcium chloride, students differentiate between endothermic and exothermic processes. This lab emphasizes the importance of thermodynamics in medical technology and sports medicine.

Module 10: Hot Ice - Supersaturated Solutions

Students investigate solubility limits and nucleation by creating supersaturated sodium acetate solutions. The resulting rapid crystallization demonstrates the energy changes associated with phase transitions and industrial heat storage.

Module 11: Drop by Drop - Intro to Titration

This module introduces analytical titration through a simplified drop-count method. Students perform neutralization reactions to determine concentrations, a core competency for pharmacy and water quality technicians.

Module 12: Lemon Power - Electrochemistry

Students construct a simple voltaic cell to explore redox chemistry and electrical potential. By powering an LED with fruit, they connect chemical reactions to renewable energy and battery technology.

Module 13: Glow Stick Kinetics

This lab investigates the relationship between temperature and reaction rate. By manipulating chemiluminescence, students visualize collision theory and its applications in safety lighting and biochemical research.

Module 14: Crushed vs Whole - Surface Area

Students quantify the effect of surface area on reaction speed. This exploration of physical states and reaction rates provides insight into pharmaceutical design and industrial milling.
