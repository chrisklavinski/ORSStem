The Pan-Texas University STEM Scavenger Hunt: Statewide Field Operations

A comprehensive, field-based curriculum designed for in-person campus visits to Texas public universities, specifically tailored for TRIO Upward Bound and UBMS programs. These site-specific modules transform standard college tours into rigorous STEM data collection missions. Students engage with geographic, architectural, and research assets at UT, A&M, UH, UNT, and Texas State systems. The curriculum is fully aligned with 34 CFR Part 645 regulations regarding rigorous math/science curriculum and exposure to postsecondary education.

Target: High School (Ages 14-18) • Total Time: 90 Minutes per Module (Site-Specific) • Est. Cost: $0.00

Module: UT Austin (The 40 Acres Topography Audit)

Duration: 90 Minutes TRIO: Both

A site-specific audit of the University of Texas at Austin. Students navigate the distinct elevation changes of the '40 Acres' to analyze civil engineering challenges, focusing on the Dell Medical School bridge infrastructure and the LEED Gold certified Student Activity Center.

Learning Objectives

  • Calculate the 'slope percent' of the walkway leading from the stadium to the main mall.
  • Locate the Gates Computer Science Complex and identify the 'Bridge' connecting the North and South distinct buildings.

Notes & Observations

The Pan-Texas University STEM Scavenger Hunt: Statewide Field Operations - Curriculum Narrative Document

Executive Summary

The Pan-Texas University STEM Scavenger Hunt is a rigorous, field-based curriculum designed to transform traditional college visits into high-impact academic missions for TRIO Upward Bound and UBMS students. By utilizing the physical campuses of the University of Texas, Texas A&M, and other major Texas systems as living laboratories, students engage in authentic data collection, civil engineering audits, and logistical modeling. This curriculum bridges the gap between postsecondary exposure and academic mastery, requiring students to apply mathematical reasoning and scientific observation to real-world campus infrastructure. The program aims to increase college enrollment in STEM majors by demystifying elite research environments through hands-on, site-specific challenges.

Compliance & Alignment

DOE TRIO Compliance Strategy

This curriculum is meticulously structured to meet the regulatory requirements for 'rigorous math and science curriculum' and 'exposure to postsecondary education' under the Higher Education Act (HEA) Section 402C(b) and 402E(b). The operations align with 34 CFR Part 645.11 regarding allowable services for Upward Bound and UBMS programs, specifically facilitating campus visits that provide career and academic counseling. Financial expenditures for transportation and educational materials are justified under EDGAR/2 CFR 200 cost principles, including 2 CFR 200.475 (Travel) for transportation to flagship institutions and 2 CFR 200.453 (Materials and Supplies) for field-based data collection tools, ensuring all activities are necessary, reasonable, and allocable to the grant‚Äôs objectives of increasing postsecondary attainment.

Standards Alignment

The curriculum is strictly aligned with the Texas Essential Knowledge and Skills (TEKS) and the Texas STEM Framework. Specific alignments include TEKS 130.362 (Engineering Science) for documenting civil infrastructure, 111.43 (Mathematical Models) for collecting and analyzing geometric data in the field, and 127.14 (Career Preparation) for conducting structured site visits. Furthermore, the modules support the Texas STEM Framework‚Äôs Domain 4 (Community and Industry Partnerships) and Domain 6 (Instruction through Experiential Learning), ensuring that students develop the technical competencies required for success in the Texas STEM workforce.

Field Expeditions

The curriculum leverages high-value STEM destinations within 60 miles of Dallas, Tarrant, and Johnson counties, providing accessible and impactful field operations. Key sites include the University of Texas at Dallas (UTD) in Richardson, which serves as a hub for Computer Science and robotics; the University of Texas at Arlington (UTA) in Tarrant County, focusing on Civil Engineering and Nursing; and the University of North Texas (UNT) in Denton, which offers specialized audits in Green Chemistry and Acoustical Physics. These locations provide diverse research environments that represent various tiers of Texas higher education, allowing programs to minimize travel time while maximizing student exposure to professional-grade laboratories and high-tech campus infrastructure.

Module Narratives

UT Austin: The 40 Acres Topography Audit

This module challenges students to analyze the civil engineering and architectural transparency of the UT Austin campus. Students apply trigonometric calculations to determine slope percentages for ADA compliance and investigate the HVAC and structural design of the LEED Gold-certified Engineering Education and Research Center (EERC), connecting academic math to real-world urban planning.

Texas A&M: The College Station Logistics Challenge

Set on the state's largest land-grant campus, this module focuses on Agricultural Science and Mechanical Engineering. Students utilize graph theory logic to optimize travel routes between West Campus research plots and the Zachry Engineering Complex, simulating the logistics efficiency required in industrial engineering and large-scale agricultural operations.

University of Houston: The Third Ward Energy Circuit

Focusing on the proximity to the Houston Energy Corridor, this activity emphasizes Chemical and Petroleum Engineering. Students explore the 'Powerhouse' campus to analyze fluid power systems through campus fountain flow rates and architectural geometry, providing direct exposure to high-demand careers in the energy sector.

University of North Texas: Green Chemistry & Acoustics Audit

This module explores the intersection of Physics and Environmental Science. Students use decibel meters to conduct an acoustics audit of the Murchison Performing Arts Center and evaluate the university's 'Mean Green' sustainability initiatives, including wind turbine energy and LEED-certified building markers.

UT Dallas: The Richardson Tech-Corridor Cipher

Located in the heart of North Texas‚Äôs Silicon Prairie, this module focuses on Computer Science and Robotics. Students interact with autonomous delivery systems (Tobors) and analyze the infrastructure supporting digital industries, fostering engagement with careers in Software Engineering and Network Architecture.

Universal Regional Campus: The Community Resource Scan

Designed for flexibility across regional universities like UTA or TWU, this module focuses on Health Sciences and local industry partnerships. Students conduct a 'resource scan' of science labs and student success centers, ensuring they understand the administrative and academic pathways necessary for navigating a regional university system.
